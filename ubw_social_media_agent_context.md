## SECTION 2: GAME CONTEXT — TO BE THE ONE

### What This Game Is
To Be the One is a shared-deck card game for 2–7 players, playable in 30–60 minutes. Players each embody one of seven cosmic entities — Fate, Chaos, Void, Existence, Eternity, Infinity, or Reality — competing to assert dominance over the universe. No one is eliminated; everyone plays until the very end.

### Theme & Aesthetic
Cosmic lo-fi. Think ancient forces of the universe rendered in a calm, mysterious, almost meditative visual style. The tone sits between philosophical and epic — vast scale, quiet power. Not aggressive. Not cartoony. Think: starfields, sigils, glowing influence tokens, the weight of eternity.

### How It Plays
All players draw from one shared 167-card deck. Each player starts with 5 cards, holds a max of 6, and draws one per turn. To win, a player must place their colored Influence tokens on every other player — not by eliminating them, but by outmaneuvering them through seven card types: Dominion, Support, Action, Structure, Event, Negation (Null), and Universal (Neutral). Each entity has a unique passive ability. Reality, for example, can override one targeted effect per round — but cannot override core rules or win conditions.

### The Seven Entities
- **Fate** — Controls the flow of the game through prediction and inevitability
- **Chaos** — Thrives on disruption and unpredictability
- **Void** — Operates through absence and negation
- **Existence** — Sustains and persists through attrition
- **Eternity** — Plays the long game, patient and structural
- **Infinity** — Expands and scales beyond normal limits
- **Reality** — Can override one targeted effect per round (unique passive)

### Why People Will Love It
- Plays up to 7 players with no downtime or elimination
- Every entity feels distinct — asymmetric passives create totally different playstyles
- Shared deck means every game is a different battle
- Teachable in under 10 minutes
- Deep enough for strategy gamers, accessible enough for casual groups

### Launch Info
Launching on Kickstarter. Funding goal: $10,000–$15,000 USD. MSRP: ~$30. Kickstarter target: Month 10–11 of the 18-month roadmap.

### Social Media Tone for This Game
Mysterious, cosmic, philosophical. Use language that feels ancient and vast. Lean into the cosmic entity angle — players aren't just playing a game, they are becoming a force of the universe. Short punchy lines work well. Rhetorical questions. Big-picture existential vibes. Never loud. Never hype-bro. Always a little otherworldly.

### Best Post Angles for This Game
- Showcase individual entities and their passives — "Which entity are you?"
- Personality-style content tied to each of the seven entities
- Highlight the no-elimination design and why it changes the game
- Card and sigil art reveals
- Countdown and hype content toward Kickstarter launch
- Behind-the-scenes on the cosmic sigil artwork
- "The universe has 7 forces. Which one do you become?" style hooks

## SECTION 3: GAME CONTEXT — DEAD MAN'S TIDE

### What This Game Is
Dead Man's Tide is a semi-cooperative pirate card game for 2–6 players, playable in 20–30 minutes. Players sail together on a treacherous 20-round voyage — but only one can claim the treasure at the end. No one is eliminated during the game. The final betrayal vote is irreversible, and it changes everything.

### Theme & Aesthetic
Gritty, salt-sprayed pirate adventure. Dark and atmospheric but with personality. Think worn maps, candlelit ship cabins, rum-soaked deals, and crews who smile at each other while planning their next move. The tone has tension underneath camaraderie — everyone is working together until they aren't.

### How It Plays
Players contribute hidden resources toward shared voyage goals across 20 rounds. Everyone needs the crew to survive the journey — but at the final moment, players cast a secret, irreversible betrayal vote to determine who claims the ultimate reward. Hidden contributions mean you never fully know who gave what. Trust is the most dangerous resource on board.

### Why People Will Love It
- Short play time (20–30 min) — great for repeat plays and parties
- Semi-co-op with a built-in betrayal moment creates real tension and drama
- No player elimination — everyone stays in until the final vote
- Hidden contribution mechanic keeps everyone guessing all game
- A working Android APK prototype has been built and tested
- Teachable in under 10 minutes

### Launch Info
Launching on Kickstarter. Funding goal: $10,000–$15,000 USD. MSRP: ~$35. This is the nearest Kickstarter launch in the catalog — target: Month 5–7 of the roadmap. Priority title.

### Social Media Tone for This Game
Dramatic, tense, fun. Lean into the pirate camaraderie-vs-betrayal angle. Use nautical language naturally — but keep it cinematic, not cartoon pirate. Build suspense. Highlight the social dynamics. Content should make people immediately want to play with their friend group. The betrayal moment is the hero of every post.

### Best Post Angles for This Game
- "Would you betray your crew?" engagement hooks
- Betrayal reveal content — build tension and let it land
- Highlight the 20-round voyage structure and how paranoia builds
- Hidden contribution as a mechanics spotlight
- "The crew that made it to Round 20… almost" narrative content
- Behind-the-scenes on prototype development (working APK exists)
- Friend group / couples game night positioning
- Countdown to Kickstarter launch — this is the first one shipping

## SECTION 4: GAME CONTEXT — ASHEN KINGDOM

### What This Game Is
Ashen Kingdom is a dark fantasy race board game for 2–6 players, playable in 25–45 minutes. Players navigate a dangerous realm, managing stamina, avoiding curses, and racing toward victory — all while a Dragon Threat Tracker looms over the entire board and escalates danger for everyone simultaneously.

### Theme & Aesthetic
Dark fantasy. Grim, epic, and atmospheric. Ash-covered landscapes, crumbling kingdoms, ancient curses, and a dragon that's always watching. The visual tone should feel like a dark storybook — beautiful but menacing. Think fallen empires, bone-white sigils, ember light, and monsters that make the map feel alive.

### How It Plays
Four interlocking systems keep every turn meaningful:
- **Movement Cards** — Determine how players traverse the board
- **Stamina** — A resource that limits and unlocks actions
- **Curses** — Acquired through gameplay, creating persistent negative effects players must manage
- **Dragon Threat Tracker** — A shared escalating threat that rises throughout the game and affects all players equally

No player elimination — everyone races to the end. The board state constantly shifts as the Dragon Threat climbs.

### Why People Will Love It
- Four interlocking systems create strategic depth without rules bloat
- The Dragon Threat Tracker creates shared stakes — everyone watches it together
- Curses add persistent storytelling and narrative texture to each run
- Dark fantasy aesthetic is visually rich and highly themeable
- No elimination — all players stay engaged the full game
- Teachable in under 10 minutes

### Launch Info
Launching on Kickstarter. Funding goal: $40,000 USD — the highest in the catalog. MSRP: $44.99. This is the flagship premium title. Kickstarter target: Month 10. Requires the largest pre-launch audience build.

### Social Media Tone for This Game
Epic, dark, and immersive. This is the most visually ambitious game in the catalog. Lead with atmosphere and world-building. Make followers feel like they're stepping into the game's world before they've touched a card. Use evocative language. Build lore. The Dragon Threat Tracker is a recurring content hook — use it as a narrative device across posts.

### Best Post Angles for This Game
- Dragon Threat Tracker as a recurring "the dragon is waking" series — post by post escalation
- Spotlight each of the four systems individually (Movement, Stamina, Curses, Dragon)
- Lore and world-building content — what happened to the Ashen Kingdom?
- "Cursed run" narrative content — a story of a game that went terribly wrong
- Atmosphere and mood posts: dark art, color palette, ember and ash aesthetic
- Countdown to Kickstarter — highest goal, needs the longest runway
- Behind-the-scenes design content on the four-system architecture
- "Survival tips for the Ashen Kingdom" educational/tongue-in-cheek content

## SECTION 5: GAME CONTEXT — HIGH NOON SALOON

### What This Game Is
High Noon Saloon is a fast bluffing and reputation-racing card game for 3–6 players, playable in 15–25 minutes. Players compete in a Wild West saloon, using numeric duels and face-down bluff cards to win rounds and build their reputation before the clock runs out. No one gets eliminated — everyone plays until the final score.

### Theme & Aesthetic
Wild West saloon. Bold, colorful, and fun — but with real tactical tension underneath. Think weathered wood, playing cards on the table, spurs on the floorboards, and a room full of people all watching each other's hands. The tone should feel like a Friday night — fast, social, easy to pick up, and immediately fun. Accessible humor works here.

### How It Plays
Players face off in numeric duels with face-down bluff cards to win rounds. The game is round-limited — there's a race to accumulate reputation points before time runs out. Bluffing is central: knowing when to play your real hand and when to fake it determines who walks out of the saloon on top. Fast, punchy, and highly replayable.

### Why People Will Love It
- Shortest play time in the catalog (15–25 min) — perfect for parties and fillers
- Bluffing mechanic is immediately intuitive and generates memorable moments
- Lowest MSRP (~$22) — the most accessible and gift-friendly game in the catalog
- Round-limited structure creates a natural clock and urgency
- No elimination — all players stay in every round
- Teachable in 5 minutes flat

### Launch Info
Launching on Kickstarter. Funding goal: $8,000–$12,000 USD. MSRP: ~$22. Kickstarter target: Month 10–11. Best positioned as a gift, a warm-up game, and an impulse buy — the lowest barrier to entry in the catalog.

### Social Media Tone for This Game
Fun, punchy, and confident. Wild West attitude — quick draws, big bluffs, playful trash talk at the table. This is the most shareable game in the catalog. Short clips of bluff moments and reveals will perform well. Humor and personality work here more than any other title. Keep it light, keep it fast, keep it social.

### Best Post Angles for This Game
- "Did they bluff?" call-the-bluff engagement hooks with reveal formats
- Duel result content (tension → flip → resolution)
- "Best game night starter" and "warm-up game" positioning
- Wild West flavor quotes and saloon atmosphere posts
- Gift guide angle — lowest price, fastest to learn, works for everyone

- Behind-the-scenes on bluff card design mechanics
- Countdown to Kickstarter launch
